Nehwal has achieved many victories in badminton. She has got over 15 medals in all. In 2012 London Olympics she won a Bronze medal in Women’s singles. She has also been ranked as number one player in the year 2015 and is currently on the 9th position in the world.

Saina Nehwal, the daughter of Harvir Singh Nehwal and Usha Rani Nehwal, was born in Hisar. She has only one sibling, an elder sister named Chandranshu Nehwal. Her father, who has a PhD in agricultural science, worked at Chaudhary Charan Singh Haryana Agricultural University. She completed her first few years of schooling at Campus School CCS HAU, Hisar. She finished 12th grade from the St. Ann's College for Women, Hyderabad.

In 2009 she was ranked as the World No. 2. It was only in 2015 that she became the top-ranked player for the first time and a lot happened in between. 2010 was the year of reckoning as Saina won gold and silver in separate events at the Commonwealth Games. Along with that was a bronze at the Asian Championships. But 2012 was the year that Saina created history when she won an Olympic bronze. It was the first time an Indian shuttler had won an Olympic medal. Sindhu would do one better in 2016 when she clinched silver. From then on there was no looking back. Be it battling injury, fighting setbacks and lack of form, Saina just kept on going. In 2018 she won 2 golds at the Commonwealth Games and it proved she was showing no signs of stopping.
