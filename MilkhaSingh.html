Who doesn’t know him? We cannot forget this track and field athlete popularly known as the ‘Flying Sikh’. He battled in 1960 Olympics but missed the bronze medal by just 0.1 seconds. However, he broke the 400m Olympic record in the same race. He also won Gold medals in the year 1958 and 1962 Asian Games.

Milkha Singh was born on 20 November 1929 into a Rathore Rajput Sikh family. His birthplace was Govindpura, a village 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) from Muzaffargarh city in Punjab Province, British India (now Muzaffargarh District, Pakistan). He was one of 15 siblings, eight of whom died before the Partition of India. He was orphaned during the Partition when his parents, a brother and two sisters were killed in the violence that ensued. He witnessed these killings.

He won the Padma Shri Honour in 1959. He won a gold medal in the 1958 Asian Games in 200m and 400m. He won a gold medal in the 1958 Commonwealth Games in 440 yards. He won a gold medal in the 1962 Asian Games in 400m and 4x400m relay.
